Definitions

  • Intestate -

    Of or relating to a person who has died without a will.

  • Testate -

    Having left a valid will at death.

  • Terms for a deceased individual

    • Decedent -

      Name for the individual who dies intestate.

    • Testator -

      A person who made a will.

    • Settlor -

      Name for the individual who dies in a trust context.

  • Terms for people who inherit property

    • Heir -

      A person who, under the laws of intestacy, is entitled to recieve a decedent's intestate property.

    • Devisee -

      A person designated in a will to recieve a devise.

    • Beneficiary -

      A person designated in a trust to receive a benefit.

  • Devise -

    When used as a noun, a testamentary disposition of real or personal property.

  • Descendant -

    The children and all other subsequent generations originating from the deceased.

  • Will -

    An instrument or declaration by which one directs the disposition of one's property after death.

  • Testament -

    Another term for Will.

  • Trust -

    A document by which one directs the control and disposition of one's property.

  • Codicil -

    Amendment to an existing will.

  • Estate -

    Property owned by the deceased individual.

  • Estate Residue -

    Property that remains after all devise property is distributed.
